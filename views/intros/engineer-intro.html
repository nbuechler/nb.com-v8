Not only do I want to think about a system in terms of its modularity, but I want to create a balance between the parts. The whole of the system should be greater than the sum of its parts. I want each of piece of the whole to have closed systems where each interacts and thrives from each other.
I strive for the perfection in the harmony, at least where the system requires a prolonged sense of tranquility. But, to be less poetic, I feel that a perfectly engineered system accomplishes its goals - no more or less.
I've learned that spillover effects in a system, even if accidentally beneficial do not enhanced the goals of the engineer. In fact, a planned and designed system is one where it benefits the user.
To this end, I seek to hone my crafts. Society will be able to benefit as it has from engineers in our society. I do not take this duty lightly, because I aim to build upon the achievements of those giants that came before me.
When others squander their opportunities, they stall attempts that humanity makes at achieving more fulfilling and more meaningful lives for its members. I have opportunities to engineer tools, applications, aesthetics, and more. I plan to make the most of what I know.
