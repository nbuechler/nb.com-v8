When I was young - around the age of 5 - I found out that I really enjoyed drawing things. In fact, I have memories of trying make sense of the universe around me through drawings of space, the planets, video game characters, and nature.
I have positive memories of art classes in elementary school. As I got older, I began to hone my craft.
My classes taught me to draw what is there, not what I think is there.
Realizing that I could communicate with art, I began to wonder what ideas I could form.
Attempting to show mortality or high school angst, I found an interest in skulls. But, my craft was enhanced to a point where these basic fundamentals are now useful in a larger way, and even useful in my current pursuits. Along this path, I've learned about other forms of art, too.
