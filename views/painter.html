Experiencing a lot of discomfort while painting was part of my process. I never really liked getting my hands super messy. I also never really liked the amount of maintenance involved with carrying everything involved, but I wasn't aware of this preference when I was in high school.
I remember feeling excited that I got to paint, and the annoyances of the easel were just part of the deal.
My skill with painting was never really all that good to begin, but I really enjoyed the abstraction of impressionism. The concept of layering items was foreign when I began. But, I've found that layering the ideas of the painting physically and metaphorically are important. Also, I discovered that different kinds of painting makes me feel different - watercolor painting makes me feel most relaxed.
I cared about the experimentation process of painting, and how different it is from other forms of art.
For me, painting something meant trying to negotiate with the canvas. It taught me some patience, and I think I'm more in touch with my inner calm because of my initial experiences with this challenging medium.
