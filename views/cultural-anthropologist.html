Relativistic thought was is something I hold with high regard, but I began to refine my own sense of empathy as I learned about other cultures.
I am disgusted by the idea that other cultures cannibalize their dead. Understandably, in some of those cases the consuming community also might see it as a disgusting, but necessary, activity. When I learned that rituals like these are important to groups of people, I felt lucky that I had another way to relate to others, even if I felt uncomfortable in the process.
I'm becoming better at understanding my gaps in knowledge or perspective. And, it makes me constantly consider what might be invisible to me but opaque to others. I try to understand that when fights occur, both parties might simply be afraid or concerned. And in some cases, this concern might be for the other. I will always be learning how to be compassionate.
I hope that others can become more relativistic because I think their lives will become easier and less confusing. If someone can overcome cognitive dissonance, then that person will probably generate a more healthy outlook on their surroundings.
We are forced to act if we want to see changes, and understanding human culture is a useful perspective in crucial scenarios. The unstable circumstances in human history usually require leaders to moderate their followers.
