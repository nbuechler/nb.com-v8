In high school I was involved with the school newspaper, and I remember treating the experience like a real job! I even decided to interview community members to report on the events going on around the high school. In college, I decided to take more of an editorial stance. At UMBC, I exercised my opinionated voice, and it was only for a semester. Afterwards, I started blogs and I am now more freelance.
Rigorous journalism is like rigorous science for me, and without rigor, I have no interest. The reporting medium has the potential to influence the population it impacts.
I've learned in these experiences that journalism can come in many forms, and particularly. the medium changes the way the message is received. I also now think about ideas as signals, and I wonder what would occur if messages were always received as intended.
While most of my ideas about journalism started with me simply trying to be heard, I now view journalism as a decentralizing force. Social media may make it possible for others to be heard, or it might quiet their voice.
While language and images are ambiguous at times, those are clearly continuing to evolve the ways in which people communicate.
