Deciding to be more minimal in my fine arts, I discovered I had an interest in design. And, wanting an internship my senior year of high school, I had a fabulous set of mentors. Being told to make something for the community, I not only designed the visuals, but also the plan of how to integrate graphics with text.
After being able to tell a story, I felt confident in my narrative abilities. I wanted to ensure that I left a legacy at my high school, and I was passionate about the community that nurtured my growth. In fact, Scorpion Success, as it was called, became a guide for other students and featured my peers.
My abilities to enhance a community improved, but my abilities to self-teach grew more. I discovered my passions in design, and set myself in philosophies forged in the heat of youth. This mindset is of optimism and a yearning to use my skills for others.
I refuse to make negative public relations material, because I notice the dangers of visual lies. Graphic design is a medium that allow a mass production of ideas, where lies can easily be conveyed in the place of truths.
I believe that humanity should only be receiving the kind of propaganda which focuses our attention towards greatness in our next healthy step. When I learned to design graphics, I also discovered the part of myself compelled to go forth, and I am committed to achieving that journey.
